Lisbon Visit

Travel

Lisbon, the capital of Portugal, a gorgeous city near the sea. Known for its red rooves, old castle encompassing the city, mountains and impeccable view. I had the pleasure of visiting April for 3 days.

It was a tepid 20º for most of our stay and thankfully we saw no rain (in contrast to Paris where we travelled from and needed an umbrella everywhere). We checked into the Big Blue Hotel in the Lisboa region. We got in late so went straight for food wating some portugese cuisine from the famous Tabuaria do Paço. I went for Octopus salad and it was ... interesting. Would recommend trying it. We headed back to the Big Blue Hotel for some well needed shuteye after our train from Paris.

We started bright and early next day and went to visit Belem Tower, a tower set out in water and it was busy. There were so many people lapping up the view and sat on the waterfront. We made our way to the Rua Augusta Arch and took a traditional tram to the Sao Jorge Castle. My legs were dead by then and we munched on some Pastel de Nata, a delicacy that is popular everywhere but native to Portugal. They have flaky pastry and a custard filling and they are just as good as they sound.

We joined a walking tour next day and saw some of the tourist attractions. We opted to get the train over to the Jesus statue over the knock off golden gate bridge. Seriously if you saw this thing in a photo you may mistake it for the San Francisco landmark. We ate Arroz de pato this evening at a local restaurant. A duck rice customary to Portugal. This was delicious, I would highly recommend.

I went solo on the next day for a day trip to Sintra a complex with many castles and breathtaking views. I wish Jay came and shared this but he was dead on his feet so opted to stay back for a day. I don't think my pictures did it justice.

If you're thinking about visiting, just do it (not a nike ad). It's spectacular and has different vibes to so many other European cities.
